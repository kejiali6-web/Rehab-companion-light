Rehab Companion Light

A pressure-sensing rehab companion for stroke survivors.

Project Overview

Rehab Companion Light began with a personal story: watching my grandfather struggle through repetitive stroke rehabilitation sessions. Many exercises involved simple grip motions with little feedback or emotional support.

This project uses an FSR-402 pressure sensor, LEDs, audio feedback, adjustable thresholds, and 3D-printed components. Below is the full week-by-week engineering build log.

Build Timeline

Week 1 – Story, Problem, and Research

  • Documented real rehabilitation observations.
  • Created the initial problem statement and design constraints.
  • Researched FSR-based sensing and stroke recovery methods.

Week 2 – Early Guidance & Architecture

  • Reviewed feasibility and design scope with engineering faculty.
  • Outlined the first system architecture diagram.
  • Drafted BOM and modular design plan.

Week 3 – First Working Circuit

  • Assembled breadboard prototype with FSR-402.
  • Developed fixed-threshold LED & buzzer feedback code.
  • Validated consistent real-time pressure readings.

Week 4 – Lab Collaboration

  • Met with Dr. Gulick for technical and feasibility review.
  • Received confirmation of project value & engineering direction.
  • Defined concrete design milestones for the next phase.

Week 5 – Team Formation & Adaptive Logic

  • Formed a multidisciplinary team (BioE + EE).
  • Designed adaptive threshold logic for progressive difficulty.
  • Prepared integration plan for voice module.

Week 6 – Potentiometer & CAD Development

  • Added a potentiometer for user-adjustable difficulty.
  • Began Fusion 360 modeling of the grip ring (lower half).
  • Focused on ergonomics & FSR protection geometry.

Week 7 – Audio Feedback & Printing Prep

  • Integrated voice-recording module.
  • Completed CAD preparation for main housing box.
  • Determined initial print parameters & materials.

Week 8 – First 3D Print & Failure Analysis

  • Printed first prototype using PLA.
  • Identified PLA rigidity issues for hand-grip comfort.
  • Decided to reprint with TPU for flexibility.
