Engineering Compassion

Empowering stroke survivors with assistive devices that respond to effort — not just motion.

— Kejia Liu

My Story

My grandfather, once a proud and independent man, lost sensation on his right side after a stroke. Recovery was no longer just about movement — it was about reclaiming dignity.

“比起害怕永远失去对身体的控制权，我更害怕的是丧失了生而为人的自尊与独立。”

Through caring for him, I witnessed how rehabilitation challenges go beyond physical limits — they test a person’s patience, pride, and hope.

What I’m Exploring

Core Problem

Current rehab tools lack human-centered design.

Technical Frontier

Exploring glucose nanosensors & soft robotics for assistive wearables.

Market & Empathy

Interviews, factory visits, and real patient stories.

What I’m Building

My vision is to create assistive medical tools that blend empathy and engineering — empowering patients to regain independence, one small motion at a time.

This is the beginning of my public build log — documenting how empathy becomes engineering.
