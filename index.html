Rehab Companion Light

A pressure-sensing rehab companion for stroke survivors.

Intro Timeline Gallery Next Steps

Project Overview

Rehab Companion Light began with a personal story: watching my grandfather struggle through repetitive stroke rehabilitation sessions. Many exercises involve simple grip motions with little feedback or emotional support.

This project uses an FSR-402 pressure sensor, LEDs, audio feedback, a potentiometer for difficulty control, and 3D-printed components. Below is the full build log documenting each week of progress.

Build Timeline

Week 1 – Story, Problem, and Research

  • Documented real rehab observations.
  • Created initial problem statement and constraints.
  • Reviewed relevant FSR-based sensing research.

Week 2 – Early Guidance & Architecture

  • Discussed feasibility with faculty.
  • Outlined first system architecture.
  • Drafted BOM and module diagram.

Week 3 – First Working Circuit

  • Built the breadboard prototype with FSR-402.
  • Wrote the first fixed-threshold Arduino code.
  • Validated real-time sensing feedback.

Week 4 – Lab Collaboration

  • Met with Dr. Gulick to review the concept.
  • Received validation on feasibility and meaningful impact.
  • Defined clear engineering goals.

Week 5 – Team Formation & Adaptive Logic

  • Formed a multidisciplinary team.
  • Added adaptive threshold logic to Arduino code.
  • Threshold now increases after successful squeezes.

Week 6 – Potentiometer & CAD Development

  • Added a potentiometer for adjustable difficulty.
  • Started modeling lower half of grip ring.
  • Considered ergonomics and sensor protection.

Week 7 – Audio Feedback & Printing Prep

  • Integrated voice-recording audio module.
  • Prepared CAD for the main housing box.
  • Selected 3D print materials and parameters.

Week 8 – First 3D Print & Failure Analysis

  • Printed first version in PLA.
  • Identified that PLA is too rigid for rehab use.
  • Will re-print in TPU for flexibility.
